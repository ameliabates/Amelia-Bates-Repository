Serial Position Effect Lab

The serial position effect is a phenomon in psychiology that refers to the tendency to remember the first and last items in a sequence better than the items in the middle. This is caused by two memory biases called the primacy effect and the recency effect.

The purpose of this lab is to test your memory and see if the serial position effect has occured. You will be presented with one word at a time. After the list was completed, you will be asked to recall as many words as you can any order. Spelling matters.
